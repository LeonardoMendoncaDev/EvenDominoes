Getting Started

Welcome

Dominoes is the classic double-six domino game, adapted for Even Realities G2 glasses. Navigate with scroll, play with tap, and draw with double-tap.

Launch

When you start the app, you'll see the main menu with two options:

  • New Game — Start a new round against the CPU
  • How to Play — View the quick rules reference

What This Covers

This page describes the controls, rules, and gameplay as implemented in this build.

Controls

Three gestures run the whole game:

Scroll (Up / Down) Navigate between pieces in your hand, or move through menu items.
Tap Play the selected domino piece. In menus, confirms the highlighted option.
Double Tap Draw a piece from the boneyard. On the game over screen, returns to the main menu.

Tap Behavior by Context

  • Menu: Select the highlighted menu item
  • Playing (your turn): Play the currently selected piece
  • Choose End: Tap = place on LEFT end, Double-tap = place on RIGHT end
  • Game Over: Tap = new game, Double-tap = back to menu
  • Rules Screen: Tap or double-tap to go back
Gameplay Flow

Basic Turn Flow

  1. Scroll to highlight the piece you want to play
  2. Tap to play it on the board
  3. If the piece can go on both ends, you'll be asked to choose: Tap = Left, Double-tap = Right
  4. If you can't play, double-tap to draw from the boneyard

Drawing from Boneyard

When no piece in your hand matches the board, double-tap to draw. If the boneyard is empty and you can't play, your turn passes automatically.

CPU Opponent

The CPU plays automatically after your turn. A brief "CPU thinking..." message appears while it selects its move.

Rules (Double-Six Dominoes)

The Pieces

A standard double-six set has 28 domino pieces. Each piece shows two values from 0 to 6.

Setup

  • Each player receives 7 pieces
  • The remaining 14 pieces form the boneyard (draw pile)
  • The player holding the highest double starts first

Playing a Piece

  • Match one end of your piece to an open end of the chain on the board
  • If your piece matches both ends, you choose which end to place it on
  • If you can't match any end, you must draw from the boneyard

Winning

  • The first player to empty their hand wins the round
  • If neither player can move and the boneyard is empty, the player with the lowest pip count wins
  • The winner scores points equal to the total pips remaining in their opponent's hand
Tip: Try to play pieces that match both ends of the board — this gives you more flexibility on future turns.
Tips & Strategy

Playing Smart

  • Play doubles early — they're harder to play later since both ends have the same value
  • Try to keep a variety of values in your hand to maximise your options
  • Watch what the CPU draws — if it draws often, the boneyard is running low
  • If you're ahead on points, play high-value pieces to increase your opponent's remaining pip count

G2 Display

  • Your hand is shown at the bottom of the display
  • The board chain is shown in the center — when the chain is long, only the two ends are shown
  • The selected piece is highlighted with a brighter outline and an arrow below it
  • Scores, boneyard count, and CPU hand size are shown at the top
